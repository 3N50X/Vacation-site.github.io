Explore Taniti

Taniti is a tropical paradise with something for everyone. From sandy beaches and lush rainforests to a vibrant local culture, our island offers a unique experience for all visitors.

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small, active volcano. Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000. Until a recent increase in tourism, most the Tanitian economy was dominated by fishing or agriculture.

FAQ's

  • Power Outlets: They're 120 volts, just like in the U.S.
  • Alcohol Sales: You can't buy or be served alcohol between midnight and 9:00 a.m.
  • Drinking Age: It's 18, and the rules are pretty relaxed.
  • Language: Younger folks mostly speak fluent English, but in rural areas, especially among older residents, it's less common.
  • Healthcare: There's one hospital with multilingual staff and several clinics around.
  • Crime: Violent crime is rare, but with more tourists, there's been a rise in pickpocketing and petty theft.
  • Holidays: Taniti has lots of national holidays, so many places might be closed. It's good to check ahead.
  • Currency: The U.S. dollar is used, but euros and yen are accepted too. Banks offer currency exchange, and most places take major credit cards.
